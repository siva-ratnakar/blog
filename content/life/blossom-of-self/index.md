O fool! No rules of grammar save the breath,<br>
When you must face the silent sting of death.<br>
Give up your thirst for gold and worldly pride,<br>
And let your mind in thoughts of Truth abide.<br>

The love you bear for one is brief and vain,<br>
A fleeting joy that ripens into pain.<br>
All bonds are threads that time or death shall rend in two;<br>
Seek That which neither fades nor leaves the True.<br>

Be calm with what your past deeds come to bring,<br>
Nor drown in passion's wild and fleeting sting.<br>
For lust is but a desire of rotting flesh;<br>
Do not let earthly nets your soul enmesh.<br>

The life of man is trembling and brief,<br>
Like raindrops on a lotus, shaking leaf.<br>
Know that the world is but a hungry prey,<br>
To ego, grief, and sickness and decay.<br>

When from the frame the weary soul has fled,<br>
The family shuns the body of the dead.<br>
Even the spouse, who swore a life of grace,<br>
Will shrink in terror from that rotting face.<br>

In play the child lets all his hours fly;<br>
In passion's fire the youth's best moments die.<br>
The aged man laments what used to be;<br>
None face the Truth of the Present, clear and free.<br>

Who are you? Why? O brother, pause and see;<br>
Why boast of friends or wealth or youth to be?<br>
For in a second, time can strike them low;<br>
Release the world and let its shadows go.<br>

The dawn and dusk, the winter and the spring,<br>
Are but the games that passing seasons bring.<br>
While time plays on, our lives ebb like the tide;<br>
Why let the storm of craving burn inside?<br>

Some dress in suits, some wear a grand disguise,<br>
Yet all are puppets in the passion's eyes.<br>
Though some seek ties and some from them depart,<br>
Only the steady find the blissful heart.<br>

In me, in you, one Essence lies the same;<br>
See through the veil of duality and name.<br>
Shed lust and anger, envy, greed, and pride;<br>
Find your true nature; let the storm subside.<br>

O seeker of the Self, be now set free;<br>
Through mastered mind, let light your vision be.<br>
With senses stilled and ego's clamor ceased,<br>
From all the bonds of flesh, you are released.<br>

This wonderful piece is inspired by the Sanskrit poet Adi Shankara.
